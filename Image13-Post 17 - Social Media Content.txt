Caption:
Dip two rings into soapy water and the film that stretches between them pulls itself into the leanest shape possible, using the least surface it can. Mathematicians call these minimal surfaces, and nature loves them because minimal means efficient.

The gyroid is one of the most beautiful members of the family. It is a triply periodic minimal surface, which means it repeats endlessly in all three directions of space, weaving through itself without ever touching. It contains no straight lines and no flat planes at all. Look at any point on it and you find a saddle, curving up one way and down the other at the same time. This is not just a mathematical curiosity either. The gyroid shows up in real cell membranes, in insect wings, and in the skeletons of certain sea creatures.
