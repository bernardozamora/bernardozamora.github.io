Gray-Scott reaction-diffusion

Two chemicals of different concentration react and when they diffuse they produce these organic patterns. They are calculated using two partial differential equations.

Hybrid fractal Brownian motion (fBM)

Adding several layers of 'mathematical noise' results in natural-looking patterns, like clouds or marble. Calculated by summing noise of different amplitudes and frequencies.

Differential Growth

Coral, fungi and root growth is emulated with a technique where connected points pull toward their neighbors but push away from nearby crowded areas, with new points sprouting wherever space opens up.

Gas Flow

When a gas moves past a solid object it can result in a swirling turbulent pattern or calmer 'laminar flow' depending on the speed and viscosity. 40 trillion calculations later (true number) we get this image.

Randomness with memory

While complete randomness exists at the quantum level, nature frequently uses 'smooth' randomness, like the rolling contours of terrain. This organic variation is simulated here using 'Perlin noise'.
